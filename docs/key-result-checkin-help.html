What is a Key Result Check-in?

A Key Result Check-in is a regular progress update where you record the current status of a key result. Check-ins help track momentum, identify trends, and ensure you're on track to hit your targets.

How to Create a Check-in

  1. Navigate to the key result - Open the specific key result you want to update
  2. Click "Add Check-in" - Found near the progress indicator
  3. Enter current value - What's the latest number? (e.g., 15,000 users)
  4. Add confidence level - How confident are you in hitting the target? (High/Medium/Low)
  5. Write status update - What happened this week? Any blockers?
  6. Add context - Explain changes, wins, or challenges
  7. Attach evidence - Screenshots, reports, or data sources
  8. Click "Submit Check-in" - Your update is recorded!

Check-in Best Practices

  • Check in weekly - Friday afternoons work well for most teams
  • Be honest - Report actual numbers, not what you wish they were
  • Provide context - Explain why numbers went up or down
  • Flag risks early - Use "Low confidence" to signal problems
  • Celebrate progress - Acknowledge wins, even small ones
  • Link to data sources - Make it easy to verify numbers
  • Keep it brief - 2-3 sentences is usually enough

Common Questions

Q: What if I don't have new data this week?
A: Still check in! Explain why (e.g., "Waiting for month-end report") and share what you're working on.

Q: How detailed should my update be?
A: Brief but informative. Focus on what changed and why. 2-4 sentences is ideal.

Q: What if I'm off track?
A: Be transparent! Explain the situation, what you're doing about it, and what help you need.

Q: Can I update a check-in after submitting?
A: Usually yes, but it's better to add a new check-in with corrections to maintain history.
