What is a Review Meeting?

A Review Meeting is a scheduled session where teams come together to evaluate progress on objectives and key results, discuss challenges, and align on next steps. These meetings ensure everyone stays on track and accountable.

How to Create a Review Meeting

  1. Navigate to Meetings section - Click on "Review Meetings" in the menu
  2. Click "Schedule Meeting" - Start creating your meeting
  3. Enter meeting title - E.g., "Q1 2025 Objective Review"
  4. Set date and time - Choose when the meeting will occur
  5. Add duration - How long will it take? (30 min - 2 hours typical)
  6. Select objectives to review - Choose which goals to discuss
  7. Invite participants - Add team members who should attend
  8. Add agenda items - List topics to cover
  9. Click "Schedule" - Meeting invites are sent!

Meeting Best Practices

  • Weekly cadence - For active objectives, meet weekly for quick updates
  • Monthly deep dives - Comprehensive review of all objectives and key results
  • Start with wins - Celebrate progress and achievements
  • Focus on blockers - Identify and solve obstacles quickly
  • Update key results live - Use real-time data during the meeting
  • End with action items - Clear next steps and owners
  • Keep it time-boxed - Respect everyone's time
üí° Pro Tip: Send the agenda 24 hours before the meeting so participants can prepare updates and data!

Meeting Types

Weekly Check-in (15-30 min): Quick status updates, no deep dives

Bi-weekly Review (45-60 min): Review progress, solve problems

Monthly Strategic Review (90-120 min): Comprehensive review, adjust strategy

Quarterly Planning (2-4 hours): Set new objectives for next quarter

Common Questions

Q: Who should attend review meetings?
A: Objective owners, key result owners, and anyone who can help unblock progress.

Q: What if we're behind on our objectives?
A: Use the meeting to identify why, adjust timelines if needed, or pivot initiatives.

Q: How do I keep meetings productive?
A: Stick to the agenda, focus on data, and make decisions quickly. Follow up offline on details.
